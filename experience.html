    Experience in detail

    Fieldwork, Animal Care & Husbandry

    A more in-depth look at the research, wildlife care and husbandry behind each role.

    Aardvark Honours Research — Waterberg · 2021–2022

    In 2021, during my honours research, I conducted an extensive study on how climate change was affecting aardvarks across Boschdraai and Bellevue farms in the Waterberg region.

    My work included identifying active burrows, monitoring aardvark activity using motion-activated cameras and direct observation, and studying ant and termite populations across varied terrains. I examined how different veld types influenced burrow density and assisted in setting up live capture traps, successfully contributing to the project’s first aardvark capture.

    Using telemetry and binoculars, I tracked and observed aardvarks during both day and night. My results showed that even in wetter environments, where food sources are expected to be more abundant, aardvarks are struggling to survive and have begun displaying abnormal behaviour in response to environmental changes.

    Wildlife Vet Volunteer — Johannesburg Wildlife Veterinary Hospital · 2022

    For a few days a week throughout June, September and October 2022, I volunteered at the Johannesburg Wildlife Veterinary Hospital, which focuses on the treatment and rehabilitation of indigenous wildlife rather than domestic animals.

    I worked alongside veterinarians caring for a variety of species, including genets, hedgehogs, bats, snakes and owls. My main responsibilities included food preparation, cleaning and preparing wildlife enclosures and occasionally assisting in veterinary procedures.

    I was also fortunate to work with endangered pangolins rescued from the illegal pet trade. During this time, I developed a broad understanding of animal care methods, dietary requirements, species-specific husbandry and basic veterinary procedures, while contributing to an important conservation effort.

    Seabird Rehab Intern — SANCCOB · Apr–Oct 2023

    From April to October 2023, I completed a six-month internship at SANCCOB, a leading seabird rehabilitation centre dedicated to conserving Southern Africa’s threatened seabird species, especially the endangered African penguin.

    I mainly worked in the Chick Rearing Unit, caring daily for ill, injured and abandoned penguin chicks and preparing them for release back into the wild. My responsibilities included feeding and tubing seabirds, administering oral and injectable medications, maintaining strict hygiene standards and carrying out general husbandry tasks.

    I also kept detailed records, monitored behaviour and assisted with laboratory evaluations of blood samples. I gained hands-on experience in handling techniques, weighing chicks, managing penguin eggs and calculating medication dosages under veterinary supervision. As my skills developed, I supervised the chick rearing unit, trained fellow interns and actively participated in the release of rehabilitated seabirds.

    Penguin Keeper — Two Oceans Aquarium · 2023–Present

    As a penguin keeper at the Two Oceans Aquarium, I currently care for Northern Rockhopper and African Black-footed penguins, as well as a variety of amphibians.

    My daily tasks include cleaning penguin beaches and amphibian exhibits, preparing and serving food such as fish, squid, prawns and insects, and administering medications when needed. I maintain detailed records to ensure smooth operations and to monitor each animal’s health.

    I also run penguin experiences where visitors can learn about and interact with the birds, and I give public talks to raise awareness about ocean conservation and endangered species. I have contributed to successfully raising two African black-footed penguin chicks and am expanding my skills by learning from other departments and preparing for a diving course that will open up further opportunities.
